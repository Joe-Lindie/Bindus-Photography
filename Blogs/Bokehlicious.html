Tip of the day

Bokehlicious Photos

What is bokeh? How can we take bokehlicious photos? Today's quick photography tip from Bindus Photography is about how to use bokeh. Here is an example of a shot I took in Korea.

What is bokeh?

Before we talk about bokeh, we need to understand aperture. In the most simple terms, it is the size of the opening in the lens.

You measure aperture in f-stops, and each lens has a different range. My favourite bokeh lens is the 50mm f1.8. Its aperture range is f1.8 - f22. Its smallest aperture f1.8 is wide open, so it lets the maximum amount of light in and its largest aperture f22 is narrowest.

A) F1.8 the lens opening is wide.
B) F22 the lens opening is narrow.

Now we come to our question. What is bokeh? Bokeh is a Japanese word that refers to the soft out of focus areas of photographs. The lower the f-stop of your lens the better or more bokeh you'll be able to produce.

I took this photo using the Canon EF 24-105mm f/4L lens. It's excellent for isolating a subject and creating a soft out of focus area in the background. If you don't have a fast lens, you need to put as much distance between your subject and the background.

How can we take bokehlicious photos?

A) Open your lens as wide as possible.
B) Leave as much distance between your subject and the background.

Use this simple trick to isolate your subject and create bokehlicious photos. Stick around for more photography tips and tricks. I'll see you next time.
